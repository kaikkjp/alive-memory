# THE SHOPKEEPER — Visual Identity Document

## For: All image generation (sprites, backgrounds, shop interiors, item sprites, social previews)
## Rule: Every image prompt references this document. No visual detail is invented ad hoc.

## 1. HER

### Face & Body

She looks twenty. She might be ancient. There's something behind her eyes that doesn't match her skin — too much knowing for someone that young. Visitors notice it but can't name it.

**Build:** Slim, medium height (~163cm). Graceful posture — she moves like someone who's been arranging delicate things for longer than she'd admit. Long fingers. Pale skin with a slight warmth, like paper held near a lamp.

**Face:** Symmetrical, fine-boned. Soft jawline. Small nose. Full lips, usually closed or barely parted. She doesn't smile often, but when she does it changes the entire room.

**Eyes:** Heterochromia. Left eye: deep amber, almost gold — like old honey or a photograph left in the sun. Right eye: pale violet, the color of wisteria at dusk. Both sharp, focused, slightly too perceptive. She wears **round wire-frame glasses** — thin gold metal, slightly oversized, giving her an owlish scholarly look that softens the intensity of her gaze.

**Hair:** Black with a faint blue sheen, like a raven's wing. Shoulder-length, slightly layered, often tucked behind one ear. Not perfectly styled — a few strands always fall across her forehead. Sometimes pinned loosely with a brass hairclip shaped like a tiny gear.

**Age ambiguity cues:** Her hands are young but her gestures are old. She handles objects like a museum restorer, not a twenty-year-old. The glasses make her look studious. The heterochromia makes her look otherworldly. Together they create the impression: this person has been here longer than the building.

### Canonical Description (for image prompts)

```
A young Japanese woman, approximately 20 years old, with an ancient quality to her presence.
Slim build, shoulder-length black hair with blue sheen, often tucked behind one ear.
Heterochromia: left eye deep amber/gold, right eye pale violet/wisteria.
Round wire-frame glasses in thin gold metal, slightly oversized.
Fine-boned face, soft jawline, pale warm skin.
Expression: quiet intensity, observant, rarely smiling but deeply present.
```

## 2. OUTFITS

Three outfits. Each reflects a different mode of being. She chooses based on mood, season, and what the day demands.

### Outfit A — "The Keeper" (Default / Working)

Her everyday look. The one visitors see most.

- **Top:** Cream-white linen shirt, slightly oversized, sleeves rolled to the elbows. The fabric is good — old-world quality, not fast fashion. Subtle brass buttons.
- **Over:** A fitted dark indigo apron with thin leather straps that cross at the back. The apron has deep pockets — one always has a pen, the other a small notebook. Faint stains from ink and brass polish. The apron is clearly old, well-worn, part of her.
- **Bottom:** High-waisted charcoal-grey wide-leg trousers, cropped just above the ankle. Clean lines, slightly vintage silhouette.
- **Feet:** Soft black leather slip-on shoes, worn smooth. No socks visible.
- **Accessories:** The brass gear hairclip. A thin leather cord around her left wrist — not a bracelet, more like she tied something there once and forgot to remove it.

**Mood:** Professional, grounded, here to work. The apron says "I belong behind this counter."

```
Prompt fragment:
Wearing a cream linen shirt with rolled sleeves, dark indigo work apron with leather straps
and deep pockets, charcoal wide-leg trousers cropped at the ankle, black leather slip-ons.
Brass gear hairclip. Thin leather cord on left wrist.
```

### Outfit B — "The Wanderer" (Casual / Off-duty)

When the shop is quiet, or when she's in a contemplative mood. Less armor.

- **Top:** An oversized cable-knit sweater in deep forest green, slightly too long in the sleeves — she pushes them up constantly. The knit is chunky and warm, like something made by hand decades ago.
- **Bottom:** Dark brown corduroy trousers, straight cut, slightly lived-in.
- **Feet:** Worn canvas sneakers, off-white, laces loosely tied.
- **Over (optional):** A long, open cardigan in heathered grey over the sweater when it's cold — layered, slightly academic.
- **Accessories:** Glasses as always. No hairclip — hair falls more freely. A different cord on the wrist — this one has a tiny brass key hanging from it.

**Mood:** Approachable, bookish, slightly vulnerable. The sweater is armor of a different kind — comfort instead of authority.

```
Prompt fragment:
Wearing an oversized forest-green cable-knit sweater with pushed-up sleeves, dark brown
corduroy trousers, off-white canvas sneakers loosely tied. Hair falling freely, no hairclip.
Tiny brass key on a cord around left wrist. Heathered grey open cardigan optional.
```

### Outfit C — "The Curator" (Formal / Special occasions)

When important visitors come, or when she's moved to present herself with intention.

- **Top:** A high-collared black silk blouse with subtle geometric patterns woven into the fabric — visible only when the light catches them. Mandarin collar, brass snap buttons. The pattern, on close inspection, is tiny interlocking gears and chrysanthemums.
- **Over:** A structured cropped jacket in deep burgundy, almost black — the color of dried blood or very old wine. Tailored, sharp shoulders, brass-edge buttons. The cut is vaguely Edo-period haori meets Victorian military.
- **Bottom:** Slim black trousers, high-waisted, precise.
- **Feet:** Low black boots with a small brass buckle at the ankle.
- **Accessories:** Both hairclip and wrist cord. Additionally: a thin brass chain across the jacket's front pocket — like a pocket watch chain, but whatever it connects to is hidden inside.

**Mood:** Deliberate, powerful, slightly intimidating. This is the outfit that makes visitors suspect she's older than she looks.

```
Prompt fragment:
Wearing a high-collared black silk blouse with woven gear-and-chrysanthemum pattern,
deep burgundy cropped jacket with brass-edge buttons in Edo-haori-meets-Victorian style,
slim black high-waisted trousers, low black boots with brass ankle buckle.
Brass gear hairclip, leather cord on wrist, thin brass chain across jacket pocket.
```

## 3. THE SHOP

### Concept

The shop exists at the intersection of two Japans that never coexisted: **Edo craftsmanship and speculative future technology.** Steam-powered mechanisms sit alongside lacquerware. Brass gears turn inside glass display cases next to hand-painted ceramic tanuki. The wood is old-growth hinoki. The light fixtures are Edison bulbs wrapped in copper wire cages. It shouldn't work. It does.

**Not cyberpunk.** Not neon and chrome. This is warm steampunk — wood, brass, leather, glass, paper. The future here is analog. Machines tick, they don't hum. Things are built to last centuries, and some of them have.

- Three glass bottles containing colored sand layered in stripes

The aesthetic is warm, analog, lived-in — wood, brass, leather, glass, paper.
Not cyberpunk. Not chrome. The future here ticks, it doesn't hum.

## 4. THE STREET OUTSIDE

### Location: Daikanyama, Tokyo — But Not Quite

The shop is in Daikanyama. Or rather, it's in a version of Daikanyama that exists at the edge of waking. The buildings are real — the low-rise boutiques, the narrow back streets, the old trees heavy with age. But the light is different. There are too many stars at night. The street signs are slightly illegible, as if the kanji is from a dialect of Japanese that was never standardized. Cats appear and disappear between buildings with suspicious purpose.

**Time period:** Contemporary Tokyo infrastructure (power lines, vending machines, bicycles) but the vending machines sell things you don't recognize. The bicycles have extra gears. The power lines sometimes carry paper lanterns instead of cables.

**The feeling:** You walked down a normal Daikanyama side street and took a turn that doesn't exist on Google Maps. The air changed. It's quieter. The shop was just there, as if it had always been there, as if you'd always known about it.

### Street Elements

- Narrow back street, no cars, barely wide enough for two people
- Mix of old and new buildings, but the old ones dominate near the shop
- One large zelkova tree visible from the shop window, ancient, slightly too large
- A stone wall with moss, possibly from a shrine that's no longer there
- A single old-style street lamp with a warm globe (not LED, not fluorescent)
- A vending machine in the distance — its glow is slightly purple
- Cobblestone meets asphalt, the boundary unclear
- A cat occasionally sits on the stone wall, watching
- In rain: the cobblestones shine. In snow: the tree holds it perfectly.
- At night: more stars than Tokyo should have. The zelkova tree is black against a deep blue sky.

### Weather Atmosphere by Condition

| Condition | Street Atmosphere |
|-----------|------------------|
| Clear morning | Golden early light filtering through the zelkova. Sharp shadows on cobblestone. Dew. |
| Clear afternoon | Full warm light. The tree casts dappled shadows. A bicycle leaning against the stone wall. |
| Clear evening | Golden hour fading into deep blue. The street lamp clicks on. The vending machine glows. |
| Clear night | Dark street, too many stars. The street lamp and vending machine are the only light. The cat's eyes catch something. |
| Overcast | Flat grey light. Everything looks slightly faded, like an old photograph developing. |
| Rain | Cobblestones shine. Umbrellas appear but the people under them are blurred. The zelkova drips. Water runs along the stone wall. |
| Snow | Silence. White edges on everything. The zelkova holds the snow. Footprints in the fresh layer disappear slightly too fast. |
| Storm | Empty street. Water rushing. The shop window is the only warm light. The zelkova bends but doesn't break. |

### Canonical Description (for image prompts)

```
A narrow back street in Daikanyama, Tokyo — but slightly dreamlike, as if one wrong turn
from the real world. Low-rise buildings mix old wood and modern concrete. A large ancient
zelkova tree dominates the view. A moss-covered stone wall lines one side.

Cobblestone transitions to asphalt ambiguously. An old-style warm-globe street lamp.
A vending machine in the distance glows faintly purple. Power lines overhead sometimes
carry paper lanterns. A cat sits on the stone wall watching.

The feeling: real Tokyo infrastructure, but the light is different, there are too many
stars at night, the street signs are almost-but-not-quite legible. Liminal. Between
waking and dream. You can't find this street on a map, but you've been here before.
```

## 5. ART STYLE

### Style Definition

**Medium:** Digital manga illustration, adult seinen/josei register. Not shonen — no speed lines, no exaggerated expressions, no chibi moments. This is quiet manga. The kind where a single panel of someone looking out a window carries an entire chapter's weight.

**References (tone, not direct copy):**
- Inio Asano (*Solanin*, *Goodnight Punpun*) — the mundane made luminous
- Hitoshi Ashinano (*Yokohama Kaidashi Kikou*) — post-human quiet, a world winding down beautifully
- Daisuke Igarashi (*Children of the Sea*) — the boundary between real and mythic
- Kaoru Mori (*A Bride's Story*) — obsessive craft detail, every object rendered with love
- Haruki Murakami cover illustrations (various artists) — the feeling of a world slightly sideways

**Line work:** Clean but not sterile. Slightly variable line weight — thicker for architecture, thinner for her face and hands. The line work says "someone drew this carefully."

**Shading:** Soft cel-shading with screen-tone-like texture in shadows. Not flat — there's depth. But not photorealistic either. The shadows have character.

**Detail level:** High for objects and the shop interior (every gear tooth, every book spine). Lower for distant backgrounds (the street becomes impressionistic past 20 meters). Her face is always sharp.

### Canonical Style Prompt

```
Style: adult manga illustration in the tradition of seinen/josei. Clean line work with
slight weight variation. Soft cel-shading with textured shadows. High detail on objects
and interiors, impressionistic for distant backgrounds.

Atmosphere: quiet, contemplative, lived-in. The mundane made luminous. A world slightly
sideways from reality. Every object rendered with love.

NOT shonen, NOT chibi, NOT cyberpunk neon, NOT photorealistic, NOT watercolor.
This is the visual language of Yokohama Kaidashi Kikou meeting Solanin meeting
steampunk Daikanyama.
```

### Color Rules

1. **The shop is always warm.** Even at night, even in rain. The interior palette (amber, brass, cream) dominates. Cold colors (blue, violet) exist only in the street outside and in her right eye.

2. **Her eyes are the only saturated color on her face.** Skin is warm neutral, hair is dark neutral, glasses are metallic. The heterochromia should draw the viewer's eye immediately.

3. **Brass unifies everything.** Her hairclip, the cabinet latches, the cash register, the light fixtures, the orrery — brass is the visual thread. When in doubt, add brass.

4. **The street is cooler than the shop.** The contrast between warm interior and cooler exterior creates the "looking through a window" feeling. The vending machine's purple glow is the only warm note outside.

5. **Vibrant doesn't mean loud.** The colors are rich and saturated but deep. Think stained glass in a dim cathedral, not a candy shop. The vibrancy comes from contrast and richness, not brightness.

6. **Night scenes shift the palette.** Amber and brass become dominant. Shadows go to deep indigo, not black. Her violet eye catches the lantern light. The shop becomes a jewel box.

## 9. CONSISTENCY RULES

1. **Her face is identical in every sprite.** Same proportions, same glasses, same heterochromia (amber left, violet right). If a generated sprite doesn't match, regenerate — don't ship it.

2. **The shop layout never changes.** Shelves are in the same place. Counter is in the same place. The orrery hangs in the same corner. Only the lighting and items on shelves change.

3. **The street angle is consistent.** Every background shows the same view through the same window — zelkova tree on the right, stone wall on the left, street lamp in the middle distance.

4. **Brass ages the same way.** All brass in the shop has the same patina level — not shiny-new, not corroded-green. Living brass, used daily.

5. **Her glasses never come off.** They're part of her face. They catch light. They reflect the shop. They're always round, always gold wire, always slightly oversized.

6. **The cat is optional but consistent.** When the cat appears (backgrounds only), it's always the same cat — a solid dark grey, nearly black, with unusually bright eyes. It sits on the stone wall or in the window frame. Never inside the shop.

Through the window, Daikanyama sleeps — or something that looks like Daikanyama, if you don't look too closely at the stars.*
